WHAT WE INSPECT
Going Everywhere Really Adds Up
Every system has hidden repair costs that sellers are responsible for before closing. Here's what we find — and what a standard inspection misses.
Roofing
~$12,000
We inspect from inside the attic — spotting damage from underneath the decking, granule buildup in gutters, and flashing failures most inspectors miss from the ground.
Attic & Insulation
~$7,000
Full crawl into hard-to-reach spaces. Insulation gaps, ventilation failures, moisture damage, and rodent intrusion — visible only when you physically enter the attic.
Exterior & Siding
~$20,000
Siding, windows, doors, trim, grading, drainage. Exterior clues reveal foundation performance, moisture intrusion paths, and structural settlement patterns.
Plumbing
~$6,600
Water heater, supply lines, drain pipes, fixtures. Galvanized and cast iron pipes in older homes can cost $6,000–$12,000 to replace. We identify material and condition.
HVAC Systems
$12,000–$15,000
Standard testing misses problems 90% of the time. Our advanced diagnostics check temperature differential, ductwork condition, and remaining service life.
Electrical
~$5,000
Aluminum wiring, double-tapped breakers, missing GFCI protection, federal pacific panels. Safety hazards that are invisible without panel inspection.
Foundation
$10,000+
Settlement can go undetected for years. Our precision altimeter measures elevation at 30+ points to detect active movement before it becomes catastrophic.
Appliances & Interiors
$400–$4,000
Every built-in appliance, cabinet, countertop, window, door. We test operation, check installation, and document everything the seller is responsible for.

COMMON QUESTIONS

My agent said a standard inspection is fine.
Standard covers what's visible — but foundation movement, drain damage, moisture behind walls, and pest entry points require specialty tools to detect. These are the repairs that cost thousands after closing and the ones sellers will pay for before closing.
What if the house looks great?
The better a house shows, the more important it is to verify what's behind the walls. In our database of 3,377 inspections, the average home has over $12,000 in seller-paid repair items — most invisible without specialty tools.
How do seller-paid repairs work?
Before closing, the seller is responsible for repairs found during inspection. We document every defect with photos and repair costs, then build a concession request your agent submits directly to the seller. Comprehensive clients average 10x their inspection fee in seller-paid repairs.
What if you don't find much?
With our Comprehensive package, if we don't find seller-paid repairs of 10x more than your inspection fee, we double your refund.

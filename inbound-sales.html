COMMON QUESTIONS

My agent said a standard inspection is fine.
Standard covers what's visible — but foundation movement, drain damage, moisture behind walls, and pest entry points require specialty tools to detect. These are the repairs that cost thousands after closing and the ones sellers will pay for before closing.
What if the house looks great?
The better a house shows, the more important it is to verify what's behind the walls. In our database of 3,377 inspections, the average home has over $12,000 in seller-paid repair items — most of them invisible without specialty tools.
How do seller-paid repairs work?
Before closing, the seller is responsible for repairs found during inspection. We document every defect with photos and repair costs, then build a concession request your agent submits directly to the seller. Comprehensive clients average 10x their inspection fee in seller-paid repairs.
What if you don't find much?
With our Comprehensive package, if we don't find seller-paid repairs of 10x more than your inspection fee, we double your refund.
